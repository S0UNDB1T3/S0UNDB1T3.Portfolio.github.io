P.5.js Portrait, DIY photoshop, & Game

p5 : DIY (Mario Themed) Photoshop using p5.js

p5. DIY Photoshop Site
My artistic practice revolves around the convergence of digital art and interactive experiences, and I am delighted to present my latest creation, the P5 DIY Photoshop—a Mario-themed exploration of creativity through nine meticulously crafted brushes. Inspired by the beloved world of Mario, this interactive digital artwork combines nostalgia and innovation, inviting participants to unleash their creativity within a whimsical realm. From pixelated mushrooms to Goombas, each brush serves as a tool to transform the digital canvas, empowering users to experiment with various artistic techniques and express their unique voice. Rooted in the DIY ethos, this artwork fosters a sense of agency and ownership, bridging the gap between art and technology while igniting joy and imagination.

p5: Game Sketch and implementation 

p5: Game Sketch
In "35 Points," I invite you to embark on an interactive experience that combines the thrill of a game with the exploration of tactile sensations. This P5.js game sketch invites players to immerse themselves in a captivating quest where the objective is simple yet profound: touch and find the ball to accumulate 35 points.
As an artist, I am fascinated by the intersection of technology and human interaction. Through the medium of P5.js, I sought to create an engaging environment that encourages players to rely on their senses and physical engagement rather than solely relying on visual cues or passive observation. By emphasizing touch as the primary mode of interaction, I aimed to forge a deeper connection between the player and the digital realm.

p5: Code made into a portrait

p.5 self port Code (.TXT) download
In my exploration of digital art and self-expression, I have embarked on a creative journey using P5.js and simple shapes to capture the essence of my self-portrait. P5.js, a powerful JavaScript library, has provided me with a unique canvas to translate my inner thoughts and emotions into visually captivating forms.
By reducing my self-portrait to basic geometric shapes, I aim to delve into the fundamental aspects of my identity. Each shape represents a facet of my being, and through their arrangement and interplay, a visual narrative emerges. This minimalist approach allows me to communicate complex ideas and feelings in a concise yet evocative manner.
The simplicity of shapes, such as circles, squares, and triangles, enables me to focus on the underlying essence rather than intricate details. In their unity, I find the unity of my own self, a harmony that transcends physical appearance and delves into the realm of the abstract. Through these forms, I aim to convey the universality of human experience and evoke a sense of connection with the viewer. .
